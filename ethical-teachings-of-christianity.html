Christianity is an Abrahamic monotheistic religious beliefs, proclaiming that Jesus Christ was elevated from the dead and is the Son of God, whose coming as the Messiah was prophesied in the Hebrew Scriptures (called the Old Testament in Christianity) and chronicled in the New Testament. It is the globe's largest and most widespread faith with over 2. 4 billion followers, comprising around 31. 2% of the world population. Its followers, called Christians, are approximated to comprise a majority of the population in 157 countries and territories. Christianity continues to be culturally varied in its Western and Eastern branches, and doctrinally varied worrying validation and the nature of salvation, ecclesiology, investiture, and Christology. The creeds of different Christian denominations usually hold in usual Jesus as the Kid of God—-- the Logo designs incarnated—-- who ministered, experienced, and died on a cross, yet climbed from the dead for the salvation of humankind; and described as the gospel, meaning the "good news". The four canonical gospels of Matthew, Mark, Luke and John define Jesus's life and trainings as preserved in the very early Christian custom, with the Old Testimony as the gospels' reputable history. Christianity started in the first century, after the death of Jesus, as a Judaic sect with Hellenistic influence in the Roman province of Judaea. The adherents of Jesus spread their belief around the Eastern Mediterranean location, regardless of significant oppression. The inclusion of Gentiles led Christianity to gradually separate from Judaism (2nd century). Emperor Constantine I legalized Christianity in the Roman Realm by the Ordinance of Milan (313 ), later convening the Council of Nicaea (325) where Early Christianity was combined into what would certainly come to be the state religious beliefs of the Roman Realm (380 ). The Church of the East and Oriental Orthodoxy both split over differences in Christology (5th century), while the Eastern Orthodox Church and the Catholic Church separated in the East–-- West Schism (1054 ). Protestantism divided right into many religions from the Catholic Church in the Improvement age (16th century). Complying with the Age of Exploration (15th–-- 17th century), Christianity increased throughout the globe using missionary job, ministration, immigration and comprehensive profession. Christianity played a noticeable role in the advancement of Western people, particularly in Europe from late classical times and the Center Ages. The 6 significant branches of Christianity are Roman Catholicism (1. 3 billion individuals), Protestantism (625 million-900 million), Eastern Orthodoxy (230 million), Oriental Orthodoxy (60 million), Restorationism (35 million), and the Church of the East (600,000). Smaller sized church communities number in the thousands despite efforts towards unity (ecumenism). In the West, Christianity continues to be the leading religion even with a decrease in adherence, with around 70% of that population determining as Christian. Christianity is expanding in Africa and Asia, the world's most populated continents. Christians continue to be substantially persecuted in numerous regions of the globe, specifically between East, North Africa, East Asia, and South Asia.
